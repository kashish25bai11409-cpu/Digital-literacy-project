# Case Study: Phishing Attack

## Introduction
Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. It usually happens through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear to come from trusted organizations like banks, online payment services, or popular companies.

## How the Crime Typically Happens
A phishing attack usually takes place in several steps:

1. The attacker sends a fake email or message that appears to come from a trusted source such as a bank or payment application.
2. The message often creates a sense of urgency by claiming that the user’s account has been blocked or that suspicious activity has been detected.
3. The message contains a link asking the user to verify their account or update their information.
4. When the victim clicks the link, they are redirected to a fake website that looks similar to the original website.
5. The victim enters their login credentials, passwords, or OTP on the fake website.
6. The attacker collects this information and uses it to access the victim’s real account.

## Who is Usually Targeted
Phishing attacks usually target people who frequently use online banking, payment applications, or email services. Students, employees, and elderly individuals are often targeted because they may not easily recognize fake messages or websites.

## Consequences
The consequences of phishing can be serious. Victims may lose money from their bank accounts, have their personal data stolen, or become victims of identity theft. In some cases, attackers may also misuse the stolen information to carry out further fraud.

## Conclusion
This case study shows the importance of being careful while clicking unknown links and sharing personal information online. Users should always verify the authenticity of emails and websites before entering sensitive details and should follow safe online practices to avoid becoming victims of cybercrime.
