# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist

Digital safety is important for everyone, especially students who frequently use online platforms, social media, and digital payment apps. The following checklist provides simple and practical steps to stay safe online.

## Safety Tips

1. **Use strong passwords** – Create passwords that include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable information like your name or birthdate.

2. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** – Activate two-factor authentication for important accounts such as email, social media, and banking apps for extra security.

3. **Do not click suspicious links** – Avoid clicking links in unknown emails, SMS, or social media messages that ask for personal or financial information.

4. **Never share OTP or banking details** – Banks and payment apps never ask for OTP, PIN, or passwords through calls or messages.

5. **Verify UPI payment requests carefully** – Always check the name and details of the person before approving any UPI collect request.

6. **Avoid public Wi-Fi for financial transactions** – Do not perform banking or payment transactions using unsecured public Wi-Fi networks.

7. **Keep your devices updated** – Regularly update your phone, apps, and antivirus software to protect against security vulnerabilities.

8. **Limit sharing personal information online** – Avoid sharing sensitive personal details such as phone numbers, addresses, or ID numbers on public platforms.

## Reporting Cybercrime

If you become a victim of cybercrime or notice suspicious activity, report it immediately through:

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- **Cybercrime Helpline:** 1930

Quick reporting can help prevent financial loss and assist authorities in taking action against cybercriminals.
